# Zima Blue — Design Context

This document captures the design principles, brand identity, and aesthetic direction for Zima Blue. All design decisions should align with these guidelines.

---

## Design Context

### Users
- **Artists/creators**: Self-taught and professional illustrators browsing their own work, organizing collections, and managing their portfolio. They need a calm, focused environment that lets their art shine.
- **Visitors**: Art enthusiasts exploring illustrations, discovering collections, and potentially contacting the artist. They seek a pleasant browsing experience with moments of discovery and delight.
- **Admin (artist)**: Managing the entire gallery—uploading images, creating collections, reviewing messages, and tracking analytics. They need efficient, intuitive controls.

### Brand Personality
**Artistic • Elegant • Satisfying**

- **Artistic**: The gallery celebrates handmade digital illustrations. The interface should feel crafted, not templated. Every interaction should honor the creative spirit.
- **Elegant**: Clean lines, generous spacing, refined typography. Nothing cluttered or rushed. The design should breathe and let the artwork be the hero.
- **Satisfying**: Micro-interactions should feel delightful—smooth animations, responsive hover states, and subtle visual feedback. Users should enjoy the act of using the interface.

### Aesthetic Direction

**Visual Tone**: Minimalist with warmth. Clean layouts with purposeful moments of color and motion. The interface should feel modern without being cold, sophisticated without being austere.

**Typography**:
- Primary fonts: Montserrat (titles), Gambetta (text), Chillax (body) from Fontshare
- Large, confident headings with light-weight body text
- Typography should feel elegant and readable at all sizes

**Color Palette**:
- **Light mode**: Clean whites and soft grays; generous use of white space
- **Dark mode**: Rich dark grays (`gray-900`), not pure black
- **Accent colors**: Cyan/pink/yellow gradients used strategically for interactive moments (login hero, small accents, hover states)
- **Rainbow interactions**: Subtle color shifts on small interactions—buttons, links, hover states—to add personality without overwhelming
- Both light and dark modes should be equally polished

**Spacing & Layout**:
- Generous whitespace to let illustrations breathe
- Mobile: 3-column masonry grid with varied aspect ratios
- Desktop: Draggable/resizable grid with fluid positioning
- Sticky headers that compact on scroll

**Interactions & Motion**:
- Smooth, purposeful animations (fade-in-up, scale transitions)
- Hover states with subtle scale (105-120%)
- Rainbow color accents on small interactive elements
- View transitions for smooth image navigation
- Respect `prefers-reduced-motion` for accessibility

**References (aesthetic inspirations)**:
- Clean portfolio sites with bold typography
- Gallery apps with intuitive grid layouts
- Sites with subtle micro-interactions that reward exploration

**Anti-References (what to avoid)**:
- Corporate/enterprise aesthetics (sterile, button-heavy, overly structured)
- Overly flashy or noisy designs (excessive animation, loud colors, cluttered layouts)
- Purple gradients (explicitly excluded)
- Dark/moody aesthetics that feel heavy rather than calm
- Minimalism so extreme it feels cold or impersonal

### Design Principles

1. **Artwork First**: Every design decision should prioritize the illustrations. The interface exists to showcase art, not compete with it. Use generous spacing, subtle backgrounds, and restrained UI elements.

2. **Elegant Simplicity**: Favor clean layouts over complex ones. If an element doesn't serve a purpose, question its presence. Elegant doesn't mean empty—it means intentional.

3. **Satisfying Interactions**: Small moments matter. Hover states, transitions, and feedback should feel polished and delightful. Users should enjoy the journey, not just reach the destination.

4. **Inclusive by Design**: Support both light and dark modes equally. Provide high-contrast mode for accessibility. Respect motion preferences. Ensure keyboard navigation works throughout.

5. **Calm with Curiosity**: The base experience should feel calm and focused. Add moments of discovery and color (rainbow accents, smooth transitions) that spark curiosity without disrupting the peaceful atmosphere.

6. **Handmade Feel**: Avoid overly templated or generic patterns. Small details—the way a dropdown appears, the curve of a corner, the timing of an animation—should feel considered and crafted.

---

## Quick Reference for Design Decisions

When making design decisions, ask:

- [ ] Does this let the artwork shine, or compete with it?
- [ ] Is this elegant and intentional, or cluttered/unnecessary?
- [ ] Does this feel satisfying to interact with?
- [ ] Does this work in both light and dark mode?
- [ ] Does this respect accessibility needs (contrast, reduced motion)?
- [ ] Is this calm with a hint of curiosity, or corporate/flashy?
- [ ] Does this feel handmade and considered?
